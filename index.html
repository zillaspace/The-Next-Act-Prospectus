Aspen Film Capital Campaign · Internal Playbook

Behind the Curtain

The working playbook for The Next Act: how to present the campaign, the house gesture that ties it together, and the ideas held in reserve. For the board and the campaign team, not for donor distribution.

Presentation Strategy

How To Sell It

One

Lead With Legacy

Start every conversation with preserving a living piece of Aspen history, not with square footage.

Two

Match Donor Identity To Space

A filmmaker belongs in a screening room. A writer belongs in the café. A family foundation belongs in the atrium. A social host belongs at the bar.

Three

Create Scarcity

Use purchased opportunities and limited founder slots to create momentum. Scarcity is essential in a market like Aspen.

Four

Make Recognition Beautiful

Use bronze, stone, leather, glass, archival photography, and warm light. Recognition should feel like a private club, not a municipal plaque board.

Suggested House Gesture

The Permanent Toast

Create a signature non seasonal drink that lives with the theatre forever.

The Next Act

Champagne or sparkling wine, elderflower, a small citrus twist, and a single Aspen leaf garnish when available. Serve it at donor events, premieres, and the grand reopening.

For non alcoholic service, use sparkling mineral water, elderflower, white grape, and citrus.

A Toast To The Next Act

Elegant, repeatable, and tied to every opening night.

Reserve

Held For Later

Ideas parked for a later discussion. Kept out of the donor prospectus so the ask stays clean, retained here so nothing is lost.

Limited Membership

Founders of the Next Act

A limited membership program creates urgency and gives donors a club level experience without competing with the formal naming rights.

10 Members
Visionary Founders

$250,000 each. Permanent recognition, private dinner, opening gala honors, and first access to signature events.

25 Members
Producer Founders

65 Members
Community Founders

New Concepts

Ideas Worth Adding

The Aspen Story Archive

Record oral histories from donors, filmmakers, artists, locals, and longtime Aspen families. Over time this becomes a cultural asset that outlives the campaign.

Legacy Seats

Offer premium seat plaques in select locations at $10,000 to $25,000. Keep this controlled so it remains elegant and scarce.

The Festival Club

A private donor salon during festivals and special programming. This can support sponsorship, membership, and major gift cultivation.

Director's Salon

An intimate space or program for filmmaker conversations, script readings, and private screenings.

The Legacy Galleries

Upgrade the Art Walls into curated installations featuring film photography, local artists, and Aspen history.

Opening Night Society

A recurring donor experience with premieres, champagne receptions, and first look programming.
